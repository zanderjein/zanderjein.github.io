About me, the things I'm reading, and my interests

About

Hi everyone! My name is Zander, and I'm currently a student at Yale University, studying economics and mathematics. I previously did a lot of competitive debating, played in orchestra, and dabbled a bit in philosophy. However, my current fixation is on the U.S. health care system—and what can be done about it.

Reading

Books, articles, and essays I'm working through right now, with a few notes of my own.

  • An American Sickness

    Elisabeth Rosenthal · Book

    A clear-eyed tour of how American medicine became a business. Reframed how I think about where the money actually goes.

  • The Cost Conundrum

    Atul Gawande · The New Yorker

    Still the essay I'd hand anyone who wants to understand why two similar towns can have wildly different health costs.

  • Being Mortal

    Atul Gawande · Book

    Less about policy, more about what care is for. A good counterweight to all the spreadsheets.
